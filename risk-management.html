Risk Management

Risk management is the single most important skill for traders. Below are core ideas and practical rules.

Core rules

  • Risk per trade: define a fixed percent of account (e.g., 0.5–1%) you're willing to risk.
  • Position sizing: determine quantity based on stop distance and risk amount.
  • Risk/Reward: have objective targets but don't force trades without edge.

Position sizing example (practice)

  1. Account size: $10,000
  2. Risk per trade: 1% = $100
  3. Stop distance: 20 pips / ticks → calculate position size so that stop loss equals $100

Journaling & expectancy

Keep a trade log and compute expectancy. Expectancy = (win% * avgWin) - (loss% * avgLoss)

Record your trades and review weekly — risk management keeps you in the game.
