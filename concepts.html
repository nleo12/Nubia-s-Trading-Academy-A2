Key Concepts (7)

These core concepts are the foundation of the curriculum. Each concept below includes practice suggestions.

1. Market Structure

Identify highs, lows, trend direction, and structural breaks across timeframes. Practice: label structure on charts.

2. Price Action Setups

Single-bar signals, break-retest, and pattern recognition. Practice: compile examples in your journal.

3. Risk Management

Position sizing, stop placement, and managing max drawdown. Practice: run simulated P&L scenarios.

4. Confluence

Combine levels, indicators, orderflow, and volume for higher probability setups. Practice: only take trades with 2+ factors.

5. Momentum & Indicators

Use momentum tools (RSI, MACD) to confirm moves — not to override price action. Practice: test indicator filters.

6. Psychological Biases

Awareness of loss aversion, confirmation bias, overtrading, and emotional exits. Practice: keep a psychology log.

7. Trading Plan & Journal

Clear rules for entries/exits, risk, and review. Practice: set weekly goals and perform post-trade analysis.

These concepts will be further illustrated with charts in later milestones.
