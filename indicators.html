Technical Indicators

Indicators are mathematical calculations applied to price, volume, or both to provide additional context. Below are commonly used indicators and how they're typically applied.

Common indicators

  • Moving Averages (MA) — Smoothing price series to find trend direction.
  • Relative Strength Index (RSI) — Momentum oscillator showing overbought/oversold conditions.
  • MACD — Trend and momentum measure using EMA differences.
  • Volume Profile / OBV — Volume-based indicators that show participation.

How to use indicators (practical)

  1. Use indicators as context, not a standalone signal.
  2. Seek confluence: price action + indicator agreement = stronger edge.
  3. Prefer simple setups — avoid overfitting many indicators.

Example: RSI + MA confluence

Use RSI divergence near a moving-average support to identify low-risk entries. (Practice on multiple timeframes.)
